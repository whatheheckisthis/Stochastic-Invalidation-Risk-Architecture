## Academic use — prohibited applications

**This material must not be used to underpin coursework content.**

This prohibition applies specifically to the following uses:

- Submitting analysis, methodology, formulae, or narrative
  derived from this suite as original work in any assessed
  coursework, assignment, dissertation, thesis, or examination
- Using this suite as a primary reference or citation basis
  for academic papers without independent verification of
  every claim against primary sources
- Reproducing model assumptions, working formulae, or
  governance structures from this suite in academic work
  without attribution and without independent validation
- Treating governance documents in this suite as authoritative
  academic literature — they are practitioner artefacts, not
  peer-reviewed publications

**The reason for this prohibition is not restrictive. It is protective.**

This suite is a practitioner-grade governance and model risk
artefact. Its analytical content — distribution rationale,
BSM derivation framing, LGD proxy construction — is grounded
in referenced primary sources (Haugh 2016, CREDIT_RISK_LAYER.md,
ASD ISM, SR 11-7). Those primary sources are the appropriate
academic citations. This suite is the application layer over
those sources, not a substitute for them.

Using this suite to underpin coursework without tracing claims
to their primary sources would produce academically weak work
that misrepresents the provenance of its analytical foundations.
That outcome is not in the interest of the student, the
institution, or the integrity of the subject matter.

## What this suite is

This suite is a practitioner-grade model risk documentation
package produced as an independent proof-of-concept. It is:

- A demonstration of governance artefact production standards
  for quantitative risk models
- A reference implementation of SR 11-7 model documentation
  conventions in an independent context
- A portfolio artefact demonstrating cross-domain depth in
  SecDevOps assurance and quantitative credit risk governance

It is not:

- A peer-reviewed research publication
- A certified compliance attestation
- A regulatory capital model
- A live risk system
- A textbook or course material
